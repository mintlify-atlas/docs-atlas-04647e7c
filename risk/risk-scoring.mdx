---
title: "How MantleScope calculates your risk score"
description: "A breakdown of the two-component scoring algorithm that converts your MNT and ETH balances into a 10–95 risk score, updated on every block."
---

MantleScope converts your live on-chain balances into a single integer between 10 and 95. The score combines two independent penalty components — price risk and concentration risk — and is recalculated every time new price data or a balance change arrives from Mantle Network. A lower number means higher risk; a higher number means your portfolio is safer under current market conditions.

## The scoring formula

```javascript
function calcScore(mnt, eth) {
  var total = mnt * S.mntPrice + eth * S.ethPrice;
  if (!total) return 72;
  var conc = mnt * S.mntPrice / total;
  var pr = S.mntPrice < 0.3 ? 35 : S.mntPrice < 0.5 ? 25 : S.mntPrice < 0.8 ? 15 : S.mntPrice < 1 ? 10 : 5;
  var cr = conc > 0.85 ? 35 : conc > 0.65 ? 20 : conc > 0.45 ? 10 : 5;
  return Math.max(10, Math.min(95, Math.round(100 - pr - cr)));
}
```

The score is `100 − pr − cr`, clamped to the range **10–95**. When a wallet has no balance, the engine returns a neutral default of **72** (low risk) rather than dividing by zero.

<Note>
  `S.mntPrice` and `S.ethPrice` are fetched from the CoinGecko API every 30 seconds and injected into the global state object `S`.
</Note>

## Price risk component (`pr`)

The price risk penalty reflects how close MNT is to price levels that historically precede liquidation cascades. The further MNT falls below $1.00, the higher the penalty deducted from the base score.

| MNT price | Penalty (pr) |
|-----------|-------------|
| Below $0.30 | 35 pts |
| $0.30 – $0.49 | 25 pts |
| $0.50 – $0.79 | 15 pts |
| $0.80 – $0.99 | 10 pts |
| $1.00 and above | 5 pts |

## Concentration risk component (`cr`)

The concentration risk penalty reflects how much of your total portfolio value is denominated in MNT. A portfolio with more than 85% in a single volatile asset carries substantially more tail risk than a diversified one.

| MNT share of portfolio | Penalty (cr) |
|------------------------|-------------|
| Above 85% | 35 pts |
| 66% – 85% | 20 pts |
| 46% – 65% | 10 pts |
| 45% or less | 5 pts |

<Tip>
  The minimum possible combined penalty is 10 pts (MNT ≥ $1.00, MNT share ≤ 45%), yielding a maximum score of 90. The clamp ceiling of 95 exists to accommodate any future tier additions that might reduce penalties further.
</Tip>

## Score bands

The final integer maps to one of three risk bands, each colour-coded throughout the MantleScope UI.

| Score range | Band | UI colour | Meaning |
|-------------|------|-----------|---------|
| 70 – 95 | Low risk | Green | Portfolio is well diversified and MNT price is healthy. |
| 40 – 69 | Medium risk | Amber | Elevated concentration or MNT price weakness warrants monitoring. |
| 10 – 39 | High risk | Red | Significant exposure. The AI engine recommends immediate action. |

## Liquidation risk derivation

The liquidation risk indicator shown on the dashboard is derived directly from the score. No separate calculation is performed.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Score above 70">
    Liquidation risk is displayed as **None**. Your positions are not under threat at current prices.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Score between 41 and 70">
    Liquidation risk is displayed as **Low**. A vault guard trigger price is suggested by the AI engine at 72% of the current MNT spot price.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Score at or below 40">
    Liquidation risk is displayed as **Medium**. The AI engine recommends reducing MNT exposure and enabling vault guards immediately.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
  A "Medium" liquidation risk label does not mean liquidation is imminent — it means your current portfolio configuration would sustain meaningful losses if MNT continued to fall. Use the Simulator to model specific price scenarios before acting.
</Warning>

## Edge cases

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Empty or zero-balance wallet">
    When both `mnt` and `eth` balances are zero, `total` evaluates to `0` and the function returns the neutral default score of **72** rather than attempting division. This prevents a NaN result and keeps the UI readable until real balances load.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Score floor and ceiling">
    `Math.max(10, Math.min(95, ...))` clamps the result. In practice, the minimum achievable score at the worst tier combination (pr = 35, cr = 35) is `100 − 35 − 35 = 30`, which lands in the High risk band. The clamp prevents hypothetical future tier additions from producing invalid UI states.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="ETH price in the formula">
    ETH price affects the denominator (total portfolio value) but has no dedicated penalty tier. This means holding more ETH relative to MNT dilutes concentration risk without attracting its own price-risk penalty, reflecting ETH's relative price stability compared to MNT.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
